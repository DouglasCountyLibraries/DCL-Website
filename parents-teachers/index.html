Battle of the Books

This bowl-style trivia tournament all about books is part of a national reading incentive program. Dozens of teams in grades 3-6 compete for the chance to become Battle of the Books champions.

Bark for Books

Bark for Books helps budding and struggling readers build confidence and self-esteem by reading out loud to trained therapy dogs without the stress of being judged.

Reading Buddies

This unique program matches trained teen volunteers to struggling readers, ages 6-9. Buddies meet weekly for two months to read and enjoy literacy-based games. Reading Buddies are available at the James H. LaRue Library in Highlands Ranch, Lone Tree, Parker and Philip S. Miller Library in Castle Rock. Applications are available in the children's/teen area.

Accelerated Reader

Understanding reading levels can help parents and teachers lead kids toward books that will neither bore nor frustrate, helping readers grow at each individual’s “independent” reading level.

Page to Stage

Page to Stage brings literature to life! Every year, we bring a classic tale to schools across the county in the form of a traveling theater production especially for kids in grades K-5.

Review Crew

Review Crew are library staff from your closest DCL branch, who will come to your school to recommend just-right books to your students in a fun and interactive way.
