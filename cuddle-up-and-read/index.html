Cuddle up & Read

Your baby's lifetime love of reading begins with the library.

DCL partners with area hospitals and pediatric doctors' and dentists' offices to provide new parents with a fun, meaningful introduction to the library and our early literacy services. We're committed to building bright futures by inspiring a love of reading, and no baby is too young!
Research shows that experiences with reading throughout the early years profoundly affect the development of literacy.
More than 4,000 Cuddle Up & Read gift packs are distributed to health facilities in Douglas County each year, including Sky Ridge Medical Center, Castle Rock Adventist, Parker Adventist, and UCHealth Highlands Ranch.
We partner with 30 local pediatric doctors' and dentists' offices (with new ones added annually).
DCL provides at least 10 new books every year to each of our pediatric partners' offices for use in their waiting rooms.
Cuddle Up & Read has enjoyed steady growth over the past seven years.
Reading readiness begins at birth. Early literacy is what children know about reading before they learn to read.
Our Summer Reading Program serves 14,000-18,000 participants annually, including babies. The time parents spend reading to their babies counts as time spent reading.

Build a Bright Future

If you are a pediatric health care provider in Douglas County and want more information about bringing Cuddle Up and Read to your practice, contact our Youth Literacy & Outreach Specialist at 303-688-7658.

Early Literacy Resources

Dolly Parton's Imagination Library

Through the Imagination Library, children from birth to age five receive a free book each month, sent straight to their home, that they get to keep.

OverDrive Kids

Safe e-book search for readers 12 and younger. Filter by format, subject, device or reading level. Includes read-alongs for preschoolers!

Kanopy Kids

Unlimited access to educational streaming videos for kids of all ages, as well as movies, storytime videos, classic tales, and much more.
