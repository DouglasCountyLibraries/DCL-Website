Happy Anniversary,

Highlands Ranch!

2021 marks the 40th anniversary of modern-day Highlands Ranch—but the history of this community dates back more than 100 years, to when it was first settled. In celebration of this anniversary year, enjoy a stroll back through time with some fun historical facts, articles, and photos curated by DCL’s own Archives & Local History department.

Fun Facts

What’s in a name? Employees of Kathi Brock’s advertising agency pulled an all-nighter with beer and pizza in order to name all of the communities and streets in the first phase of Highlands Ranch by a deadline of the following morning.
The Scott family became the first residents of Highlands Ranch when they moved into the Groves in October 1981. The Mission Viejo Company gave the Scotts a 650-pound steer as a move-in gift.
Cheers to Austrian immigrant Johann “John” Welte (third from right-front), who operated the Cheese Ranch from 1878 to 1910 producing Limburger and American cheeses as well as butter. His dry farming techniques were published in scientific journals of the day, and his ranch was also known as a beer hall.
Following Prohibition, local gambling entrepreneur Ova Elijah “Smiling Charlie” Stephens purchased the Fly’n B Ranch from the Plews family, known today as Fly’n B Park. After serving time for attempted murder in the ‘40s, Smiling Charlie returned to the ranch and used it as a temporary location for illicit gambling operations until losing big in 1946.

How to Implement a Master Plan

 

Forty-three years after it was first conceived, Mission Viejo’s dream has come to fruition. With more than 20,000 developed acres, Highlands Ranch is one of the largest planned communities in the United States.
