Board of Trustees

Volunteer trustees appointed by the Douglas County Commissioners adopt and oversee the budget, policies, and planning, as well as the library director's hiring and evaluation.

Terry R. Nolan
President
District III, Term Expires 12/31/2026
Appointed in January 2021. Terry Nolan served our country in the U.S. Navy for 26 years, including a combat stint in Vietnam, and achieved the rank of captain. Following his Navy career, Terry served as general manager of the Highlands Ranch Metro District for more than 24 years, where he worked for the organization’s elected board of directors, led Metro District staff, and was responsible for managing many community services provided by the organization.
Suzanne Burkholder
Vice President
District I, Term Expires 12/31/2025
Appointed in January 2020. Suzanne Burkholder has a background in business and community engagement. She is currently a professional mediator in conflict and dispute resolution and previously worked for a premium television/video programmer. She volunteers for the City of Lone Tree’s Teen Court, utilizing restorative justice and diversion principles and repairing harm. Suzanne is a parent of three, all educated in Douglas County schools. She’s been a Douglas County resident for 29 years and lives in Lone Tree with her husband.
Ted Vail
Secretary
District III, Term Expires 12/31/2025
Appointed in January 2023. Ted Vail owns a Colorado-based manufacturing and distribution business and has also run businesses internationally. He’s passionate about businesses of all types and making a positive impact on the lives of those touched by the goods and services provided. Ted is tech-savvy and embraces web-based technologies for strategic goals. He has an MBA in Finance from the University of Denver. Ted and his family live in Highlands Ranch and love checking out materials from the library.
Zach McKinney
At-Large, Term Expires 12/31/2025
Appointed in January 2023. Zach McKinney has a background in international relations, French, and political science. Now working in corporate software development, he has a passion for the intersection of technology and literacy. Zach and his spouse and two young children all relish going to Douglas County Libraries (and hate leaving). He lives in Highlands Ranch, and when not reading can be found running or biking in the backcountry.
Pam Hampton
District II, Term Expires 12/31/2027
Appointed in February 2025. Pam Hampton has a degree in Art and Business Administration from Western State College. In her accounting career Pam worked in various capacities, including payroll and as a financial analyst and staff accountant. She also operated her own picture-framing business for 20 years, served as the president and treasurer for a multistate trade association of framing professionals, and volunteered on various local committees and organizations, including Parker Chamber of Commerce. Now retired, she lives in Parker with her husband of 42 years.

Amy Windju
District II, Term Expires 12/31/2027

2024 Budget News
In response to escalating tax assessments in Douglas County, the board limited revenue growth to the rate of inflation and within the library's historic average of about 6% each year. This temporary change is expected to result in estimated savings of over $5 million for Douglas County taxpayers.
After being called into special session by Governor Polis in November 2023 over the statewide escalation of property taxes, the Colorado Legislature changed the state's property tax appraisal rates and added deductions setting back county assessor support to determine the library's 2024 revenue. Rather than completing the budget in early December 2023, the board passed the library's budget and allocated funding on January 9, 2024.
