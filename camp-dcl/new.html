Camp DCL

Summer Camps
June 2-August 1, 2025 | Ages 5-12

Explore, create, and experiment this summer at Camp DCL! Choose from our weeklong half-day camps at six DCL locations or full-day camps at select locations.

Materials and snacks are provided for all camps; full-day campers must bring a nut-free lunch daily. Please also bring your own water bottle.
Frequently Asked Questions
Castle Pines
Science of Superheroes LEGO Camp
June 16-20*, 9AM-12PM
Ages 5-10, $200
Get ready to explore the world of your favorite superheroes though the science of superpowers! Campers will learn about forces, energy, and problem-solving as they build models inspired by Superman, Batman, and other favorite heroes. In this super-educational camp, experimentation and fun are crucial! Facilitated by professionals from Snapology. Bring your own water bottle.
*Camp will operate as usual on June 19, despite DCL’s Juneteenth closure.
Farm to Table Cooking Camp
July 14-18, 9AM-12PM
Ages 5-10, $250
Going from farm to table never tasted so good! Campers will explore the bounty of summer-fresh ingredients and how those foods get from the ground to our plates. Young chefs will also learn what makes certain ingredients so super for the body while practicing basic cooking skills and techniques. Facilitated by professionals from Sticky Fingers. Bring your own water bottle.
On Sale Monday, February 3!
Castle Rock
Time-traveler Art Camp
June 2-6, 9AM-12PM
Ages 5-10, $225
Art, fashion, architecture, and people—this is an art time-travel machine! Campers will create images and crafts from ancient Egypt, Polynesian tappa cloths, prehistoric fossils, mammoths, life in Colonial America, and more using a wide variety of mediums, including watercolors, acrylics, collage, clay sculpture, and 3D. Don’t miss your chance to create, make friends, and explore amazing history through the eyes of an artist! Facilitated by professionals from KidzArt. Bring your own water bottle.
Escape Room STEM Camp
June 23-27, 9AM-12PM
Ages 8-12, $250
Escape summer boredom and have a ton of fun! Campers will solve escape room challenges and use ciphers, tricky puzzles, lock codes, a scavenger hunt, paper rooms, and more. They'll bring our "Ancient Egyptian Puzzles" experience (inspired by mythology) to test whether campers can work together to solve mysteries on a time limit. They will also work in small teams to create mini escape rooms that other campers will try to solve. Create puzzles kids can use to escape from a forgotten pyramid. Find a hidden treasure room. Unearth the secrets of an angry mummy. Anything’s possible! Facilitated by professionals from Inspire to Learn and Imagine. Bring your own water bottle.
Castle Rock Full-Day Camp
July 21-25, 9AM-4PM
Ages 5-10, $425
LEGO & Cooking Camp: Let's build skills—perfect for ninjas and bakers in training! Each morning, campers will become students of the history, culture, skills, and tactics of ninja warriors, including learning about shinobi through building activities, games, and projects. Aspiring ninjas will have a blast translating their names to Japanese and learning the importance of strength, concentration, and agility through physical challenges. After lunch, things heat up as campers learn the basics of scratch baking—pita bread to lemon tarts to cornbread. Get set to get sticky creating sweet and savory baked goods in this tasty and imaginative camp! Facilitated by professionals from Snapology and Sticky Fingers. Bring your own nut-free lunch and water bottle each day.
On Sale Monday, February 3!

FAQs
Please note: Your camper's spot is not fully secured until your payment has been processed by Douglas County Libraries and you have received a payment confirmation email.
Q: Are snacks provided, and what are they?
A: Yes, we provide a drink (usually juice and/or water), fresh fruit (an apple, small orange, etc.), and usually a nut-free prepackaged snack (such as graham crackers, animal crackers, etc.). We will let you know which specific snacks will be offered for your camp in the pre-camp email.
Q: Will you provide more information on the camp as the date gets closer?
A: Yes, a pre-camp email will be sent the week prior to your scheduled camp and it will list the dates, times, location, a brief daily description, tips or notes for the week, a contact phone number, and any additional information you'll need to review prior to the start of camp.
Q: What is your cancellation policy?
A: Cancellations & Refunds: Cancellations made up to three business days prior to Camp start will be eligible for a full refund, less a $25 cancellation fee. No refund is given for a cancellation with fewer than three business days' notice. A full refund will be given if DCL cancels a Camp for any reason. In the event of a DCL-initiated cancellation, parents or guardians will be notified by email or phone. Parents wishing to withdraw a child from Camp should provide written notification via email.
Please read our Camp DCL Policies & Procedures for more information.
