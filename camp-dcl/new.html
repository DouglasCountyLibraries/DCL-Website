Camp DCL

Summer Camps

June 5-August 4, 2023

Explore, create, and experiment this summer at Camp DCL! Choose from our weeklong half-day camps at six DCL locations or full-day camps at select locations.

Materials and snacks are provided for all camps; full-day campers must bring a nut-free lunch daily. Please also bring your own water bottle.
Frequently Asked Questions

Castle Pines

Purrr-Fect Pets Art Camp

June 19-23, 9AM-12:30PM
Ages 5-7, $180
This week is all about pets—furry, scaly, slimy, fuzzy, and slippery! Campers’ adorable pets will come to life through crafts and drawings of cats, dogs, bunnies, fish, lizards, and more. Kids will create at least three projects a day and work with a variety of art mediums, including watercolors, acrylic paint, collage, clay sculpture, and 3-D projects. They will also develop a positive, stick-with-it attitude and problem-solving skills through a unique, fun teaching approach. Facilitated by professionals from KidzArt. Bring your own water bottle.

Ocean Blue Science Camp

July 17-21, 9AM-12:30PM
Ages 5-10, $235
Dive into the world of marine biology and oceanography for an adventure under the sea! Campers will dissect a real shark, design their own fish, and bring to life genuine Triassic Triops. We’ll examine seashells from the seashore and make a cool design to take home. The fun continues when kids examine the anatomy of a fish and make fish print t-shirts. Finally, we’ll come face-to-face with some unique sea creatures, such as squid, to end our week at camp. Facilitated by professionals from Science Matters. Bring your own water bottle.

Mission to Mars Science Camp

June 26-30, 1-4:30PM
Ages 7-12, $190
3-2-1...Blast off to Mars! Campers will be transported to a whole new world during this fun week. They’ll study the red planet, learn what it will take to get us there, and create solutions for how people can live on Mars. Be part of the first manned “Mission to Mars"! Facilitated by professionals from Space Foundation. Bring your own water bottle.

Basic Training Baking Boot Camp

July 24-28, 9AM-12:30PM
Ages 5-7, $200

Kids will learn the basics of scratch baking and how to make everything from pita bread to lemon tarts, challah to cornbread, and more. Each recipe can be recreated at home for even more baking fun. Global Taste Buds Cooking Camp

June 5-9, 1-4:30PM
Ages 5-10, $200

Young chefs will cook recipes from countries all around the world and explore the customs, geography, and languages associated with each recipe. FAQs

Please note: Your camper's spot is not fully secured until your payment has been processed by Douglas County Libraries and you have received a payment confirmation email.
Q: Are snacks provided, and what are they?
A: Yes, we provide a drink (usually juice and/or water), fresh fruit (an apple, small orange, etc.), and usually a nut-free prepackaged snack (such as graham crackers, animal crackers, etc.). We will let you know which specific snacks will be offered for your camp in the pre-camp email.
Q: Will you provide more information on the camp as the date gets closer?
A: Yes, a pre-camp email will be sent the week prior to your scheduled camp and it will list the dates, times, location, a brief daily description, tips or notes for the week, a contact phone number, and any additional information you'll need to review prior to the start of camp.
Q: What is your cancellation policy?
A: Cancellations & Refunds: Cancellations made up to three business days prior to Camp start will be eligible for a full refund, less a $25 cancellation fee. No refund is given for a cancellation with fewer than three business days' notice. A full refund will be given if DCL cancels a Camp for any reason. In the event of a DCL-initiated cancellation, parents or guardians will be notified by email or phone. Parents wishing to withdraw a child from Camp should provide written notification via email.
Please read our Camp DCL Policies & Procedures for more information.
