Breakout Session Descriptions:

Introduction to Journaling

John Bosley
Location: Parker 2nd Floor Conference Room
Have you ever wanted to journal but don’t know how to get started? Then this class is for you! Journaling can have many benefits, like helping you with organization, emotions, and creativity. In this class we will discuss the different types of journals you can keep, including bullet journals, daily journals, creative journals, and tracking journals. You’ll likely discover that you can benefit from one or many of them.

Vision & Vitality: A Creative Workshop for Professional Renewal

Release Bodywork + Healing
Location: Parker Event Hall A
Join ReAnna of Release Bodywork + Healing for an immersive vision board experience designed to reset the nervous system and clarify professional intentions. Through guided visualization and creative collage, you will cultivate a visual road map for your personal and professional well-being, leaving the session feeling inspired and re-centered.

Simple Strategies that Lead to Jumbo Amounts of Joy

Lisa White, The Joy Seeker
Location: Parker Kids Corner
Often the smallest shifts in our focus can have the biggest impacts on our lives. During this interactive keynote you’ll learn proven strategies for connecting with colleagues, incorporating more joy into the workplace, and discovering a happier and more fulfilled you. You’ll come away feeling renewed and armed with simple, actionable tools that you can start using right away, both personally and professionally.

The ROI of Human Connection

Braden Eads
Location: Parker Recording Studio Nook
In a world increasingly driven by technology and efficiency, the most powerful competitive advantage your organization has is the one that can't be automated: genuine human connection. This engaging breakout session explores how the quality of your workplace relationships directly impacts performance, retention, and culture. You will walk away with practical tools to strengthen your professional relationships, communicate with more intention, and create a workplace where people feel seen, valued, and motivated to bring their best.

Finding Your Why: A Narrative Art Therapy Directive

Tonja Graves
Location: Parker Event Hall B
“Finding Your Why” is a 45-minute art therapy directive that uses visual metaphor to help individuals explore their sense of purpose and underlying motivation. Through guided reflection, you are invited to imagine your life as a journey shaped not just by events, but by the deeper reasons that drive your choices. You’ll consider what fuels you, what values guide you, and what gives your life meaning. You will then create a road using symbols to represent formative experiences, core beliefs, challenges that strengthened them, inspirations, and sources of support along the way. A structured reflection phase supports meaning-making, value clarification, and increased self-awareness. Directive goals include identifying personal purpose, strengthening internal motivation, recognizing resilience, and fostering alignment between your values and future direction.
