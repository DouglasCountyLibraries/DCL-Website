Breakout Session Descriptions

My Desk Is My Gym

Andrea Fuller
Location: Outside
One of the worst things you can do for your back and your posture is sit at a desk all day hunched over a computer. Unfortunately, that’s the reality of some jobs. Don’t settle for slouched shoulders and an achy back! Instead, learn quick and easy exercises and stretches you can do at your desk to counteract stresses and feel better about your overall health.

Balancing Your Plate & Fueling Your Body

Jessica Snyder
Location: Kid’s Corner
While we may know the importance of nutrition, it can be challenging to find time to make balanced meals. Join Jessica to discover practical ways to balance your plate and fuel your body. You’ll also learn about common nutrition pitfalls, positive impacts of meal planning, macronutrients (carbs, fats and proteins), and helpful tips for making positive behavioral changes.

Integrative Health

How your mind and your diet play essential roles in finding balance in your everyday life
Sandrine D’Andrea
Location: Event Halls A&B
Sandrine will share some real-life examples of where, when and how our minds and our diets can help us take control of our lives. She will share some easy tips along with some breathing exercises, and provide an easy introduction to meditation/relaxation.

Creating Harmony Among Generations at Work

Peggy Chase
Location: Inside, in between first floor conference room and study room
Workplace dynamics are rapidly changing. One workplace may have four generations on the same team, sharing the same space. Each brings its own values, rules and styles, which may lead to conflict or unproductive competition. This session can help participants understand generational differences and get tips for creating a harmonious work environment.

Yoga for Posers

Cassandra Severns
Location: Outside
Refresh your mind, body and soul with yoga in Civic Green Park! Join expert Yogis as they guide you through stretches, breathing exercises and meditation techniques to calm the mind. Bring a yoga mat or blanket if you have one, although it is not required. Be sure to wear something stretchy and comfortable as there will be lots of movement.

Balance Your Day Away!

Read, take a walk, or try your hand at some balancing activities!
